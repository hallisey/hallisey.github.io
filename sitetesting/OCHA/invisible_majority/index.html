What is an Internally Displaced Person (IDP)?

“Internal displacement is a situation in which persons have been forced or obliged to flee or to leave their homes, in particular as a result of, or in order to avoid, the effects of armed conflict, situations of generalised violence, violations of human rights or human-made disasters, and who have not crossed an internationally recognised state border.”

(Guiding Principles of Internal Displacement, United Nations, 1998, E/CN.4/1998/53/Add.2)

What are the causes of internally displaced persons?

Conflict and violence and lack of respect for international humanitarian law remain the root causes of displacement, but other factors such as natural disasters, climate change and, in some cases, demographic and economic growth are forcing more and more people to flee their homes. Natural disasters displaced some 19.2 million people in 2015.

Today the likelihood of being displaced by a natural disaster is 60 per cent higher than it was four decades ago. The World Humanitarian Summit issued a strong call to the support the Platform on Disaster Displacement (follow up to the Nansen Initiative) to strengthen the protection of people forcibly displaced by natural disasters, included those linked to climate change.
